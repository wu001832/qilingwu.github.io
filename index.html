About

My work examines how people respond to misinformation and correction in health and political contexts. I am interested in message design, social norms, source effects, and the role of platform affordances in shaping engagement with corrective information. I also work on projects involving AI-assisted correction and interactive communication environments.

This site will include updates on my research, ongoing projects, conference presentations, and selected materials.

Research

Misinformation Correction

I study when and why corrections work, including the effects of accuracy, plausibility, source cues, and corrective framing on beliefs and behavioral intentions.

Corrections Belief Updating Sharing Intentions

Health Communication

I am interested in theory-based message design, help-seeking communication, and the ways health misinformation circulates and is challenged across different audiences.

Health Messages Help-Seeking Audience Response

Social Norms and Platforms

My work also considers how social endorsement, perceived norms, and platform design features influence the spread of misinformation and the visibility of correction.

Social Norms Platforms Affordances

AI and Interactive Interventions

I am developing research on AI-assisted correction, chatbot-based interventions, and experimental designs that test how interactivity changes responses to corrective content.

AI Correction Chatbots Experiments

Selected Projects

AI Correction Chatbot
A prototype chatbot designed for experimental research on misinformation correction, interactivity, and message design.
Correction, Accuracy, and Plausibility
Research examining how plausibility and accuracy shape intentions to share or correct misinformation.
Platform-Based Correction Experiments
Ongoing work testing how corrections operate in social-media-like environments, including the effects of visibility, social endorsement, and interaction.
